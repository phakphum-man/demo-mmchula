Admissions

What are the qualifications of an MM candidate?

expand_more

An MM applicant must have at least a bachelor's degree (in any field). It is important that he/she have at least one year of work experience. Those who have less than one year of work experience need to show equivalent experience or related activities. Also, he/she must submit the English Proficiency score of either CU-TEP 75, TOEFL iBT 79, or IELTS 6.5.

How are candidates selected for an interview?

expand_more
Candidates are shortlisted based on their academic records, work experience, English proficiency scores, and the quality of their Statement of Purpose. Shortlisted candidates will be invited for a formal interview.

What are the admission criteria?

expand_more
The final admission decision is based on a holistic review of the application, including the interview performance, professional potential, and academic readiness for the rigorous MM curriculum.

How long are English test results valid?

expand_more
English proficiency scores are valid if they are no more than two years old (referring to the test date until the Fall semester starts). Exceptions apply to citizens of US, UK, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand who completed a degree-level course in those countries.

Tuition & Expenses

What is the tuition fee for the MM program?

expand_more
The total tuition for the entire program is approximately 459,000 THB for Thai students and 580,000 THB for International students, covering all core courses and modules.

Are there any additional expenses?

expand_more
Additional expenses may include textbooks, study materials, and the international study trip module (if selected), which varies by destination and duration.

expand_more
